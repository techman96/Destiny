Pakistani Americans are citizens of the United States with ancestry from Pakistan. The term may also refer to people who also hold a dual Pakistani and U.S. citizenship. In 2019, there were an estimated 554,202 self-identified Pakistani Americans, comprising about 0.187% of the U.S. population, and about 2.50% of Asian Americans; more specifically, around 8% of South Asian Americans, according to American Coummunity Survey report. Pakistan is the 12th highest ranked country of origin for immigration to the U.S.
